# Maturity

## Overview

Maturity measures the long-term health and sustainability of each tool's development ecosystem. This criterion is split into two sub-dimensions per the rubric. **Demonstrated Maturity (5a)** evaluates backward-looking evidence: release cadence (E-1), commit activity (E-2), issue responsiveness (E-5), CI/testing practices (E-6), and operational adoption (E-7). **Sustainability Risk (5b)** evaluates forward-looking risk factors: contributor concentration and bus factor (E-3), funding stability (E-4), and governance model. The composite tier is determined by a matrix that pulls toward the weaker sub-criterion when 5a and 5b diverge.

This criterion carries the **lowest priority** in the rubric. It does not directly affect technical capability but indicates whether a tool will remain viable over a multi-year adoption horizon. A tool that scores Strong on every other criterion but Failing on Maturity would present unacceptable program risk.

### GridCal — <span className="tier-weak">Weak</span>

</summary>

**5a (Demonstrated Maturity): Adequate.** GridCal has an exceptionally high release cadence (approximately 128 releases in 24 months, averaging 2+ per week), though most releases lack changelogs (E-1). The trailing 12-month window shows 2,217 commits across 11 human committers (E-2). Issue tracker health is a concern: 135-day median time-to-close with a batch-closing pattern and 31% zero-comment closures; an open OPF constraint correctness issue (#397) has been unresolved for 293 days (E-5). The 49 example scripts in the main repository and active commit history support an Adequate 5a assessment.

**5b (Sustainability Risk): Weak.** Bus factor is 1: the primary maintainer (SanPen) holds 73.2% of all-time commits (9,523), is the sole PyPI publisher, and is the sole repository owner (E-3). Zero merged PRs have code review (0% review rate across 29 sampled PRs). CI is non-functional for testing: only CodeQL runs; the test suite (102 test files) is never executed in any automated pipeline, and no coverage measurement exists (E-6). The funding model relies on a small commercial entity (eRoots Analytics, Barcelona, est. 2022) with an open-core model. A Linux Foundation Energy incubation application was withdrawn in January 2025 (E-4). Adoption is concentrated in the eRoots consulting ecosystem, with 18+ client organizations listed (E-7).

**Composite: Weak.** 5a Adequate combined with 5b Weak yields Weak per the rubric matrix. An organization adopting this tool would need to establish its own test, validation, and regression detection infrastructure.

## Summary

Maturity separates the field into three tiers. PyPSA and pandapower earn Strong composites through the combination of active multi-contributor development, diversified institutional funding, and confirmed operational adoption by transmission operators and regulators (E-3, E-4, E-7). PowerModels, PowerSimulations, and MATPOWER each earn Adequate, supported by institutional backing (DOE/LANL, NREL, academic) but constrained by bus factor of 1 and limited or absent production deployment (E-3, E-7). GridCal earns Weak due to the convergence of three risk factors: bus factor of 1, zero code review across all merged PRs, and a CI pipeline that never executes the test suite (E-3, E-6).

The most common risk pattern across all tools is contributor concentration. Five of six tools have a bus factor of 1, and only pandapower achieves a bus factor of 3. For any tool selected for Phase 2, an internal test and regression infrastructure would be prudent insurance against upstream maintenance disruptions.
