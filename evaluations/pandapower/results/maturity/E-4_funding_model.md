# E-4: Funding Model

## Result: PASS

## Finding

pandapower has strong dual institutional backing from the University of Kassel and Fraunhofer IEE, with additional state-level funding from Hesse. A commercial spin-off (Retoflow) provides supplementary revenue. The funding model is research-institution-based rather than commercial-subscription or donation-based.

## Evidence

**Primary institutional sponsors:**

1. **University of Kassel** -- Department of Energy Management and Power System Operation (e2n). pandapower is a core output of this research group. Prof. Dr. Martin Braun leads the department and is director of the Grid Planning division at Fraunhofer IEE.

2. **Fraunhofer IEE** (Institute for Energy Economics and Energy System Technology) -- Department for Distribution System Operation. Fraunhofer is Germany's largest applied-research organization, publicly funded with project-based industry contracts.

3. **State of Hesse** -- Through the Hessian Ministry of Science and Art (HMWK), which co-funds the KDEE (Competence Center for Decentralized Electrical Energy Supply) at University of Kassel where pandapower was developed.

**Commercial spin-off:**

- **Retoflow** -- Founded 2021 by employees of Fraunhofer IEE and University of Kassel. Offers pandapowerpro (commercial variant) with features such as an interactive network editor, integration of additional network types, and professional support. Claims 10+ distribution grid operators as customers.

**Sustainability indicators:**

- Fraunhofer institutes operate on a ~30% base funding / ~70% contract research model, providing structural stability
- The e2n group GitHub organization (e2nIEE) maintains pandapower alongside pandapipes (gas/heat networks), indicating ongoing institutional commitment
- 500,000+ PyPI downloads as of February 2024, demonstrating community demand that justifies continued funding

## Implications

The dual institutional backing from a major German university and a Fraunhofer institute provides strong funding stability. Fraunhofer's applied-research model means pandapower development is tied to ongoing grid planning contracts, not just academic curiosity. The Retoflow spin-off adds a commercial sustainability path. Risk: funding is concentrated in German public institutions, which could be affected by budget changes at the state or federal level, but Fraunhofer's diversified contract model mitigates this.
