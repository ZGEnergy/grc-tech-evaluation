# E-5: Funding Model

## Criteria

Assess the sustainability and transparency of the project's funding model.

## Result: QUALIFIED PASS

GridCal is backed by a commercial company with a viable open-core business model, but
funding details are not publicly disclosed.

### Funding Structure

- **Sponsoring entity**: eRoots Analytics, S.L. (Barcelona, Spain)
- **Origin**: Spinoff from Universitat Politecnica de Catalunya (UPC)
- **Key person**: Santiago Penate Vera, CTO of eRoots and primary GridCal developer
- **Business model**: Open-core. GridCal/VeraGridEngine is the open-source variant;
  GSLV is the commercial product offering C++-accelerated solvers and enterprise features

### Revenue Indicators

- eRoots sells GSLV (commercial variant) to utilities and grid operators
- Consulting and integration services around grid modeling
- Solar Impulse Efficient Solution label (recognition, not direct funding)
- Academic partnerships (UPC, CIRCE)

### Assessment

The open-core model aligns incentives: eRoots benefits from a healthy open-source
project as a lead-generation and credibility tool for GSLV sales. This is a proven
sustainability pattern (cf. GitLab, Elastic, Grafana).

### Qualification Reason

- No public financial disclosures or annual reports for eRoots
- Company size is small (estimated <20 employees based on contributor data)
- No foundation governance or diversified funding (unlike LF Energy projects)
- Single-company sponsorship means funding is tied to one entity's commercial success
- If GSLV sales decline, open-source investment could be reduced
