# E-4: Funding Model

## Summary

PowerSimulations.jl is funded by the **U.S. Department of Energy (DOE)** through NREL (National Renewable Energy Laboratory, now National Laboratory of the Rockies). Funding comes from multiple DOE programs, primarily the Office of Electricity's Advanced Grid Modeling (AGM) program.

## Institutional Affiliation

- **Developer:** NREL-Sienna team at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory
- **Operator:** NREL operates under DOE Contract No. DE-AC36-08GO28308
- **Organization:** NREL is a federally funded research and development center (FFRDC) under the U.S. DOE Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation

## Funding Sources

1. **DOE Office of Electricity -- Advanced Grid Modeling (AGM) Program**
   - R2D2 project (Reliability and Resilience of coordinated water Distribution and power Distribution)
   - Primary funding source cited in NREL publications

2. **DOE Office of Electricity -- Grid Modernization Laboratory Consortium (GMLC)**
   - FlexPower project
   - Under contract AC36-08GO28308

3. **DOE Scalable Integrated Infrastructure Planning (SIIP) Initiative**
   - Original umbrella program under which Sienna was developed

## Funding Model Classification

- **Type:** Government/institutional (100% public funding via DOE)
- **Stability:** High -- DOE national lab funding is multi-year and programmatic
- **Risk factors:**
  - Dependent on DOE budget appropriations and program priorities
  - No commercial revenue or diversified funding sources
  - Political risk: federal R&D budgets subject to administration changes
  - No foundation or community funding model as a fallback

## Global Recognition

- Listed by the **Global Power System Transformation Consortium (G-PST)** as an open-source tool
- Has a DOI via Zenodo (badge in README), indicating academic publication standing
- Active Slack community for users

## Observations

Government lab funding provides strong short-to-medium-term stability but introduces political risk. Unlike commercially-backed open source (e.g., pandapower/Fraunhofer) or community-driven projects (e.g., PyPSA), PowerSimulations.jl has no diversified funding model. If DOE priorities shift away from open-source grid modeling tools, there is no fallback revenue stream.
