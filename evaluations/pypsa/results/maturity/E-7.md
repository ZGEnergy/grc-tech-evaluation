# E-7: Operational adoption

## Finding

PyPSA has extensive adoption beyond academia, with documented use by government agencies, transmission system operators, energy regulators, international organizations, and private-sector energy companies. This represents one of the broadest adoption profiles among open-source power system tools.

## Evidence

### Government Agencies

- **International Energy Agency (IEA):** Uses PyPSA in its Global Energy and Climate Model for seasonal variability studies.
- **European Commission Joint Research Centre (JRC):** Converted METIS/PRIMES scenarios for the Fit for 55 package to PyPSA networks (2022); used PyPSA for MODECO energy communities study (2023) and Redispatch & Congestion Management study (2024).
- **Canada Energy Regulator (CER):** Used PyPSA for Canada's Energy Future 2023 report.
- **GIZ (German Agency for International Cooperation):** Published studies for Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, and Brazil using PyPSA.

### Energy Regulators

- **EU Agency for the Cooperation of Energy Regulators (ACER):** Building a PyPSA solution for the EU-wide flexibility assessment platform.

### Transmission System Operators

- **Austrian Power Grid (APG):** Uses PyPSA for the Austrian energy system vision to 2050.
- **TransnetBW (Germany):** Used PyPSA-Eur-Sec for 2050 grid requirements study with 90% CO2 reduction target.
- **ONTRAS (Germany):** Gas transmission system analysis.
- **ENTSO-E:** Scenario building and cost-benefit analysis.
- **TenneT (Netherlands):** Ancillary services acquisition research.
- **Austrian Gas Grid Management (AGGM):** Sector-coupled Austria model to 2050.

### Private Sector

- **Shell:** European electricity market simulations.
- **Saudi Aramco:** Renewables integration assessment.
- **Tokyo Electric Power Services (TEPSCO):** Japan power system modeling.
- **d-fine (consultancy):** Stromnetz 2050 study; European infrastructure planning.

### Strategic Partnerships

- **Breakthrough Energy (Bill Gates):** Partnership with TU Berlin to combine PyPSA-Eur-Sec with Breakthrough Energy Sciences' software framework for unified US/EU energy system planning.

### Country-Level Models

PyPSA has spawned dedicated national models maintained by various institutions:
- PyPSA-Eur (pan-European), PyPSA-USA (Stanford), PyPSA-GB (Edinburgh), PyPSA-DE (TU Berlin), PyPSA-PL (Instrat, Poland), PyPSA-Earth (global/developing nations)

## Implications

PyPSA's operational adoption by entities like the IEA, ACER, JRC, and multiple TSOs demonstrates that the tool has crossed the threshold from academic prototype to production-grade infrastructure. The use by energy regulators (ACER) for EU-wide policy platforms is particularly significant -- it indicates the tool meets the reliability and transparency requirements of regulatory decision-making. The Breakthrough Energy partnership adds strategic visibility and long-term sustainability. For an evaluating organization, this breadth of adoption reduces the risk of choosing a tool that might become abandoned or unsupported.
