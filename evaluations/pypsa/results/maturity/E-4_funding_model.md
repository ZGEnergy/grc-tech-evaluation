# E-4: Funding Model and Institutional Backing

## Finding

PyPSA is funded through a combination of German federal research grants (DFG), EU project funding, and institutional support from TU Berlin. Open Energy Transition (OET) provides commercial backing. Funding is durable but grant-dependent.

## Evidence

**Primary institutional home:** Technische Universitat Berlin (TU Berlin), Department of Digital Transformation in Energy Systems, led by Prof. Tom Brown.

**Funding sources identified:**

1. **DFG (Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft):** German federal research funding agency. DFG grants have funded PyPSA development directly. This is competitive but renewable grant funding.

2. **EU Horizon projects:** Multiple EU-funded energy research projects use and contribute to PyPSA, including references to ENTSO-E scenario tools and JRC studies.

3. **TU Berlin institutional support:** Faculty positions and PhD students working on PyPSA are funded through the university's regular budget plus project grants.

4. **Open Energy Transition (OET):** Commercial entity founded to provide professional PyPSA support, training, and development services. This adds a commercial sustainability layer beyond academic funding.

5. **PyPSA-Earth / PyPSA-meets-Earth initiative:** Funded by Climate Analytics and other organizations, extending PyPSA to developing countries.

**Funding risks:**
- Primary funding is grant-based (DFG, EU), which requires periodic renewal
- No evidence of endowment-style or permanent institutional funding
- OET commercial model is relatively new and its financial sustainability is unproven
- Loss of Tom Brown's group at TU Berlin would significantly impact development capacity

## Implications

The funding model is diversified across academic grants, EU projects, and emerging commercial support (OET). This is stronger than pure hobby/volunteer projects but weaker than corporate-backed or foundation-endowed open source. Grant cycles create renewal risk. Qualified pass: funding is adequate and multi-source, but not permanently secured.
