## Funding Model Classification

PyPSA operates under a **hybrid academic-commercial funding model**:

1. **Academic funding**: Core development funded through TU Berlin research positions and European/national research grants. Tom Brown (creator) is a professor at TU Berlin.

2. **Commercial entity**: Open Energy Transition (OET) was founded by PyPSA core developers to provide commercial sustainability. OET co-leads development of linopy, technology-data, and other ecosystem packages.

3. **Consulting revenue**: Three consultancies provide paid support, creating a commercial ecosystem around the open-source tool.

4. **Community contributions**: 104 lifetime contributors, many from universities (50+ universities documented as users).

## Sustainability Assessment

**Moderate-to-strong sustainability.** The funding model combines:
- Stable academic positions (TU Berlin professorships are tenured)
- Commercial entity (OET) providing non-grant revenue
- Broad institutional user base creating demand for continued development
- MIT license ensures community can sustain the project independently if institutional support wanes

**Risks:**
- Heavy dependence on TU Berlin/OET axis
- European grant cycles create funding uncertainty
- No large corporate sponsor (unlike some competing tools)
