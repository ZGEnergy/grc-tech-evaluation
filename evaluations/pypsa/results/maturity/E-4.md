# E-4: Funding model

## Finding

PyPSA has diversified institutional funding from multiple credible sources, including a major European research university, the German federal research foundation, Bill Gates' Breakthrough Energy, and several German federal ministries. This multi-source funding model provides strong long-term sustainability.

## Evidence

**Primary institutional home:**
- Department of Digital Transformation in Energy Systems, Technische Universitat Berlin (TU Berlin)
- Led by Prof. Tom Brown (nworbmot), one of PyPSA's creators

**Current funding sources:**

1. **German Research Foundation (DFG)** -- Grant number 528775426 supports ongoing PyPSA maintenance and development.

2. **Breakthrough Energy (Bill Gates)** -- In February 2022, TU Berlin and Breakthrough Energy announced a partnership to combine PyPSA-Eur-Sec with Breakthrough Energy Sciences' software framework. This aims to create a unified model for European and US energy system planning. ([Source](https://www.tu.berlin/en/about/profile/press-releases-news/2022/februar/net-zero-energy-system/))

3. **German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Action (BMWK)** -- Funds the RESILIENT project (Grant No. 03EI4083A), which supports PyPSA workshops and development.

4. **Helmholtz Association** -- Previously funded PyPSA development at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT) from April 2018 to March 2021 via a Young Investigator Group Grant.

**Commercial ecosystem:**
- **Open Energy Transition (OET)** -- A company providing commercial support, consulting, and development services around PyPSA. Listed on the PyPSA users page as actively contributing to the open-source project.

**Historical trajectory:**
- 2015-2017: Developed at Frankfurt Institute for Advanced Studies (FIAS)
- 2018-2021: Funded at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology via Helmholtz
- 2021-present: Primary development at TU Berlin with DFG, BMWK, and Breakthrough Energy funding

Sources:

## Implications

The multi-layered funding model is a significant strength. Academic institutional homes (TU Berlin) provide researcher continuity; government grants (DFG, BMWK) ensure baseline funding; the Breakthrough Energy partnership brings strategic visibility and resources; and the commercial support company (OET) creates a self-sustaining economic model. This is one of the most robust funding structures among open-source power system tools.
