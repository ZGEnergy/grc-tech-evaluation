# E-3: Contributor concentration

## Finding

PyPSA has moderate contributor concentration. The top 3 contributors account for 46.9% of all lifetime commits. While this is not extreme, the project does have a recognizable "bus factor" concern: the original creator (nworbmot / Tom Brown) and two key maintainers (fneum / Fabian Neumann, FabianHofmann) collectively represent nearly half of all code contributions.

## Evidence

Top 10 contributors by lifetime commits (total: 2,691 across 100 contributors):

|------|------------|---------|-------|
| 1 | fneum (Fabian Neumann) | 450 | 16.7% |
| 2 | nworbmot (Tom Brown) | 413 | 15.3% |

**Top 3 share:** 46.9% (fneum + nworbmot + FabianHofmann)

Bus factor analysis:
- The top 4 human contributors (fneum, nworbmot, FabianHofmann, lkstrp) account for 60.0% of commits
- Excluding bots, the top 3 represent ~53% of human commits
- However, the recent 12-month data shows 42 active committers, suggesting the contributor base is broadening

Mitigating factors:
- All top contributors are affiliated with TU Berlin's Department of Digital Transformation in Energy Systems
- Open Energy Transition (OET) provides commercial support and development
- The project has institutional backing from the German Research Foundation (DFG)

Sources:
- GitHub API: repos/PyPSA/PyPSA/stats/contributors
- [PyPSA Contributors](https://github.com/PyPSA/PyPSA/graphs/contributors)

## Implications

The 46.9% top-3 concentration is a moderate risk. If key maintainers were to leave TU Berlin or shift focus, development velocity could slow significantly. However, the institutional embedding at TU Berlin, DFG funding, the Breakthrough Energy partnership, and the growing contributor base (42 committers in the last year) provide meaningful resilience. The bus factor is estimated at 3-4 -- losing any single contributor would not cripple the project.
