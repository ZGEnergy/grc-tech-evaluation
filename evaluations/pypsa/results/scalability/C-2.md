# C-2: ACPF at Scale (MEDIUM -- 10,000-bus network)

## Summary

PyPSA attempted full AC power flow (Newton-Raphson) on the 10,000-bus ACTIVSg10k
network. The solver did NOT converge after 72 iterations. The Jacobian matrix
became exactly singular during the iteration process, indicating numerical
difficulties with this network at scale.

## Method

- Loaded MATPOWER case via `matpowercaseframes` + `import_from_pypower_ppc`
- Solved with `net.pf()` (Newton-Raphson nonlinear power flow)
- PyPSA internally uses `scipy.sparse.linalg.spsolve` for the Jacobian system

## Observations

- The solver hit a singular Jacobian (`MatrixRankWarning: Matrix is exactly singular`),
  preventing Newton-Raphson convergence.
- Voltage angles remained at 0.0 (initial values), confirming no solution was found.
- This is a known challenge for large-scale ACPF: the ACTIVSg10k network may require
  better initial conditions (e.g., a flat start with DCPF warm-start) or specialized
  handling of its topology (2,980 transformers, some with zero impedance).
- PyPSA's NR solver does not support warm-starting from DCPF, nor does it expose
  step-size damping or other convergence aids for difficult networks.
- The 72 iterations completed in 9.4s, showing that the per-iteration computational
  cost is manageable (~130ms/iteration), but convergence robustness is the bottleneck.
